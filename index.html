How to Use Edge Board

what everything means & how to find the best props

①What this is (read first)

Edge Board is a matchup model, not a tip service. It pulls live MLB rosters, lineups, stats, weather and (optionally) sportsbook odds, then estimates how each hitter projects against the pitcher they're facing today. Nothing here is a guaranteed winner — it's a tool to find spots where the numbers and the price disagree in your favor.

Bankroll first. Bet only what you can lose, use flat stakes, and treat the model as one input. 21+. If gambling stops being fun, call 1-800-GAMBLER.

②The tabs

Slate

Best Plays — the 12 strongest hitter matchups across the day, sorted by Edge Score. Leaderboard — every hitter ranked by a projection you choose (HR, hits, total bases, RBI, steals) plus a Pitcher Ks board. Every Game — one card per game with both starters, weather, the best play in each market, and the top bats facing each pitcher.

✓ Align — Alignment Score

Every prop gets a 0–100 Alignment Score from nine weighted factors: recent form (L5/10/15), home/away split, lefty/righty split, matchup vs the pitcher, advanced metrics, projection consensus, sportsbook line value, odds movement, and weather. The engine also returns a confidence rating, a risk level, and a factor-agreement count (how many signals back the over). Each card shows a plain-English summary and a checklist (✓ supports / ✕ against / – no data). Props where 80%+ of available factors align are highlighted. Filter by market (HR, hits, RBIs, total bases, strikeouts) and sort by alignment or best value. Factors that need data you don't have (Statcast, odds) gracefully show "–" rather than guessing.

Sims

Full-game simulations: a projected final score, total (over/under), moneyline win %, and run line cover %. The run model blends each team's last-30-day offense, the opposing starter's ERA (weighted for ~5.5 innings, league-average bullpen after), and the park + weather run environment, then plays out the score distribution. Load odds and it shows the book line and your edge for each.

Vulnerable

Starters ranked by how exploitable they are, in tiers (prime / lean / marginal), with their vs-LHB/RHB splits and the top bats to target on their weaker side. Plus a "tough — fade" list of arms to avoid.

Weather

Every game sorted into HR-favorable, unfavorable, or neutral, with the estimated % swing to home-run probability and the temp/wind behind it.

📊 Track

The reliability check. Save snapshots today's model probabilities; after games finish, Grade pulls the real box scores and scores each prediction. Over time it shows your calibration curve (does a predicted 30% actually hit ~30%?), hit-rate vs expected, and Brier / log-loss sharpness — the real measure of whether the model can be trusted. With odds saved, it also tracks flat-stake ROI on the +EV picks. Stored in your browser only, so use the hosted/downloaded page.

Props

The probability model on its own. Pick a market (1+ Hit, 2+ Hits, Home Run, 1+ RBI, 2+ Bases, 3+ Bases) and every hitter is ranked by the model's chance of hitting it, with the fair odds. Load odds and it adds the book's line and your edge.

Odds

A side-by-side comparison of every US sportsbook for a market, with the model's fair odds, the de-vig consensus, the best/worst price, and your edge. Sorted best-edge first. This is where you find soft prices. (Requires loading your odds key.)

Parlays

Build optimized parlays from the model. Choose the number of legs, the max bats from any one game (keep this low — same-game legs are correlated), how many parlays to spit out, and which props — one type or several to mix & match. Stack filters to narrow the pool: only platoon-advantage bats, only the A- to B- grade band, only hitters facing a 4+ ERA starter, or only those facing the most vulnerable pitchers. With odds loaded it shows real book payouts and parlay edge; without, model fair odds. Legs are weighted toward the strongest model spots, with randomness so each run gives fresh combinations.

Teams

Browse any team in start-time order. See the confirmed batting order 1–9 the moment it posts (full roster before that), each hitter's stats/projections/grade, and the starter's full profile including their vs-LHB/RHB splits.

Trends

Auto-generated, searchable one-liners pulled from real data — platoon splits, hot/cold streaks, batter-vs-pitcher history, team last-30-day offense, and vulnerable/HR-prone starters. Type a player, team, "vs LHP", "hot", "last 30", or "weak spot".

③What the numbers mean

The headline metrics

  • Grade (A+ → F) — overall favorability of the hitter's matchup. Blends the season matchup, career vs-pitcher history, platoon side, recent form, park and weather.
  • Edge Score (1–99) — the numeric version of the grade; how the Best Plays are ranked.
  • xHR / xHits / xTB / xRBI / xSB — projected count of each in today's game (e.g. xHR 0.42 = the model's expected home runs).
  • Model % & Fair odds — the chance of a prop hitting, and the no-vig price that probability implies.
  • Edge % — model probability minus the book's de-vig (true) probability. Positive = the book is paying more than the model thinks it should. This is the number that matters most for betting.

The context stats

  • SLG / ISO — slugging and isolated power (raw power, ignoring singles). HR% / XBH% — how often a plate appearance becomes a homer / extra-base hit.
  • BvP — that hitter's career line against today's specific pitcher (small samples are capped so they only nudge the grade).
  • EV / LA / Barrel% / Hard-Hit% — Statcast quality-of-contact (only shows if you supply a Statcast proxy or host the page).
  • Pitcher vs LHB / vs RHB — HR allowed, AVG/OPS against, and K% to lefties vs righties. HR/9 — homers allowed per nine innings.

⑤Step by step: picking the best props

1
Load the slateIt loads today automatically. Check the status line for the game count and how many lineups are confirmed — projections sharpen once real batting orders post (~2–3 hrs before first pitch).
2
Load odds (recommended)Paste your free the-odds-api.com key in the bar up top and click Load. This unlocks real Edge % and the whole Odds tab. Without it you still get projections and fair odds, just no book comparison.
3
Start at Best PlaysSkim for A / A+ grades and Edge Score 70+. These are the strongest matchups before you even look at a price.
4
Look for stacked signalsThe best spots have multiple greens lining up: ADV platoon, 🎯 WEAK SPOT, 🔥 hot form, strong BvP, a Vulnerable starter, hitter-friendly weather, a high HR park, and good ISO/Hard-Hit%. One signal is noise; four pointing the same way is a real edge.
5
Pick your market on the Props tabDecide what you're betting — homers, hits, total bases, etc. Note the Model % and fair odds for your shortlist.
6
Go to the Odds tab and sort by edgeThis is the payoff step. The table is sorted best-edge first. A positive Edge % means the model's probability beats the de-vig consensus — the price is soft. That combination (good grade and +EV price) is your best prop.
7
Shop the best priceThe best book for each player is highlighted green. Always take the highest available number — the difference between books is free money over time. Compare it to the de-vig "fair" column to see how much edge survives the juice.
8
Sanity-check the contextOpen the game card or Teams view: is the lineup confirmed? Is your hitter actually in the order and in a good slot? Is the weather/park still pointing the right way? Skip anyone not confirmed in the lineup once orders are out.
9
Bet responsiblyFlat stakes, only edges you understand, and remember a +probability is not a guarantee — variance is huge on single games. Track results and let the sample grow.

⑥Setup & data

  • Odds API key — free at the-odds-api.com. Player props may need a paid tier; if a market comes back empty that's the plan, not a bug. ~6 credits per game, all books included.
  • Statcast proxy — Baseball Savant blocks direct browser access, so EV/Barrel%/Hard-Hit% only appear if you host this page or paste a CORS proxy prefix (e.g. https://corsproxy.io/?url=).
  • Sources — MLB Stats API (rosters, lineups, stats, results), Open-Meteo (weather), the-odds-api.com (odds), Baseball Savant (Statcast). Your odds key is kept in memory only and never saved.
  • Caveats — early-season splits/BvP are small samples (capped on purpose); the weather wind in/out is an estimate from park orientation; team last-30 form is best-effort.
  • Storage & structure — saved data lives in your browser under namespaced keys: eb:preds (predictions + graded results for performance tracking) and eb:odh (odds history for line-movement). The scoring engine is modular: each factor is a self-contained function reading from a shared context bundle, so a new data provider can be added as one factor without touching the aggregation math.
